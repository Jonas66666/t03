Why Choose Energy Efficient Televisions?

Televisions account for approximately 5-10% of the average Australian household's energy bill. By choosing an energy-efficient model, you can significantly reduce your electricity consumption and environmental impact.

Understanding Energy Ratings

In Australia, the Energy Rating Label helps consumers compare the energy efficiency of appliances. The more stars, the more energy efficient the appliance is compared to other models of similar size.

Our televisions range from 4 to 6 stars, ensuring you get the most efficient technology available on the market.

Key Questions for TV Buyers

What TV screen technologies are available?

Most TVs in Australia are LCD LED, while OLED is less common.

Pie Chart: LCD LED (83.1%), LCD (10.4%), OLED (6.4%)

LCD (LED) has the highest usage in TV screen technologies in Australia at 83.14%, followed by LCD with 10.38% and OLED with 6.43%.

What screen sizes are most common?

55-inch is the most common size, followed by mid-range sizes around 42–50 inches.

Bar Chart: 55" (530 models), 42-50" range

There are around 300 screen sizes available globally, and the most frequent screen size is approximately 55 inches with 530 TV models.

Which brands dominate the market?

Samsung, Sony, and Hisense dominate the market with the largest number of models.

Brand Comparison: Samsung (859), Sony, Hisense

Samsung Electronics has the greatest number of different models with approximately 859 globally.

Which screen types use the least power?

LCD TVs consume the least power, while OLED uses slightly more.

Power Consumption: LCD (53 kWh/yr), OLED (higher)

How does screen size affect power use?

Larger TVs use more power - the larger the screen size, the more power consumed.

Scatter Plot: Screen Size vs Power Consumption

There's a clear relationship: as screen size increases, power consumption also increases.

Do larger TVs mean worse star ratings?

Star ratings are fairly distributed; bigger TVs can still be efficient.

Star Rating Distribution by Screen Size

The larger screen sizes generally have higher star ratings. However, ratings tend to be higher on medium sizes compared to small and large based on user preferences.

When choosing a TV, balance size, brand, and efficiency. A 55-inch LED TV is common and energy-smart.
